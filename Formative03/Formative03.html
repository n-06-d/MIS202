Purpose and Learning Focus

This formative assignment prepares you for the Summative03 Written Communication assignment by helping you understand how Microsoft Word is used in professional settings, not just how individual features work.

You will explore core Word tools and reflect on:

  • how document structure and formatting influence clarity and tone,
  • how professional formatting choices affect credibility and reader perception,
  • and how visible document features can later serve as evidence of effective written communication.

This is a low-stakes, preparatory assignment. You are expected to experiment, observe, and think critically-not produce a polished final document. Grading emphasizes conceptual understanding and reflection, not technical perfection.

After completing this assignment, you should be able to:

  • identify key Microsoft Word features used in professional business documents,
  • explain how text, paragraph, and page formatting influence clarity and tone,
  • recognize when and why professionals use styles, tables of contents, and proofing tools,
  • describe how collaboration and revision workflows support written communication,
  • and prepare to apply Word formatting and revision tools in a polished business document.

You are not expected to master every Word feature. Focus on understanding why and when professionals make specific formatting and revision choices.
Copilot features may not be available in your environment; understanding the concept is sufficient.
